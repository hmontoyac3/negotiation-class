Session 2 — The Rights

Integrative

You negotiated media rights. Most of you competed — but one side needed audio and one needed video. The conflict was not real.

What you learned: Positions divide. Interests connect. Ask "why" before you react to "what." The best deals create value — they don't just claim it.

The skill is knowing which situation you're in. Most real negotiations are both at once — some issues are distributive (who gets how much), some are integrative (what each side actually needs). The negotiator who can read that difference, and switch between the two modes, wins more and burns fewer relationships.
3 QUESTIONS — BEFORE EVERY NEGOTIATION
Not a framework to memorize. Three questions to ask yourself before you open your mouth in any negotiation — salary, budget, partnership, anything.
QUESTION 1
"What do I actually need — and what happens if I don't get it?"
This is your BATNA and reservation price. Know them before you start. Without them, every pressure tactic works on you. With them, you negotiate from strength — even when you feel nervous.

QUESTION 2
"What do they actually need — and why do they need it?"
This is the integrative unlock. Their position is what they're asking for. Their interest is why they want it. The win-win almost always lives in the interests — not the positions. If you don't know their interest, you're negotiating blind.

QUESTION 3
"What can I give them that costs me little — but is worth a lot to them?"
This is the value creation move. In The Offer: remote work costs HR nothing but was worth real money to the candidate. In The Rights: audio costs VisualSport nothing. Look for asymmetry — your cheap is their precious.

Where AI fits: Use it to answer all three questions before you sit down. Research the other party. Estimate their BATNA. Generate creative trades. Rehearse the conversation.
