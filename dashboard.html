What the Professor Says — Opening 3 Minutes

"I'm going to ask you a question. Think of the last time you didn't get something you wanted — a grade you deserved, a job offer that wasn't enough, a decision made without you. How many of those situations could have gone differently if you had known how to negotiate?

Negotiation is not arguing. It's not manipulation. It is the fundamental skill of getting two or more people with different interests to find a path forward. CEOs do it. Presidents do it. You do it every day without realizing it.

In the next two-and-a-bit sessions, you will do three real negotiations. You will feel uncomfortable. That discomfort is the learning."

Four Concepts That Run Everything
01

BATNA

Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement. Your walk-away option. Your real source of power. Never negotiate without knowing yours — and theirs.

02

ZOPA

Zone of Possible Agreement. The overlap between what you'll accept and what they'll offer. If there's no ZOPA, there's no deal — move on.

03

Anchoring

The first number stated disproportionately influences the final outcome. Go first when you have good information. Make it bold. Set the range.

04

Positions vs. Interests

Position = what you say you want. Interest = why you want it. The path to win-win is always through interests, never through positions.

Classroom Logistics for 120 Students

Before Each Session

  • Pre-assign pairs (random or strategic) for Ex.1 and Ex.2 before class. Post list on screen.
  • Print or share PDFs of role cards digitally — each student only sees their own role
  • For Beta University: pre-form groups of 5–6 (20–24 groups). Assign roles per group.
  • Have this dashboard open on the projector at all times for navigation
  • Keep a visible timer on screen during every exercise

Managing 120 Students

  • Use a shared Google Form or Mentimeter for real-time data collection during debriefs
  • Appleton/Baker: 60 pairs negotiate simultaneously — takes 20–25 min max
  • Call 3–4 couples to report results to class (first offer, counter, final) — 10 min debrief
  • Non-participating students: flag names. Remind at start: participation = attendance grade.
  • Ugli Oranges: most powerful when students don't realize they can share. Do NOT hint.
