You need a place to stay for 3 months.

⏺ Before you start negotiating: hit RECORD on your video call. You'll need the recording for your submission.
Your Situation
  • You have a 3-month internship starting in April and need furnished housing nearby — ideally within 20 min of the office
  • Your company gives you a housing allowance of €1,300/month
  • You found an apartment that fits perfectly — your partner in this negotiation is the owner
  • Your BATNA: a student dorm room (€900/month, but far from the office, shared bathrooms, small — you'd really prefer not to)
  • Ideally you'd move in late March to have a few days to settle before work starts
Your Numbers — Keep These Secret
€1,300 Your Ceiling The full allowance — don't spend it all if you can avoid it
€1,000 Your Target Leaves you money for food and transport
What Matters Beyond Price
  • You'd pay €1,200 (higher than your target) if the apartment is walking distance to the office
  • Flexibility on move-in date matters — you'd like late March if possible
  • You're a quiet, responsible person. This matters to landlords — use it.
  • You'll pay on time, every month, without reminders
⚠ Do Not Reveal
  • That your allowance goes up to €1,300 — if they know, they'll ask for exactly that
  • That you really don't want the dorm (it weakens your BATNA in their eyes)
  • How much you like this specific apartment
Use AI to Prepare — 10 Minutes
Copy this prompt. Read the response. Build your strategy before you get on the call.
I need to rent a furnished apartment for 3 months for an internship. My company gives me a housing allowance of €1,300/month but I'd prefer to keep some of that for food and transport — ideally paying around €1,000. My backup option is a student dorm at €900/month but it's far and uncomfortable. The owner wants to negotiate the price. Help me prepare: - What should my opening offer be? - How do I justify paying less than they want? - Should I mention the dorm as an alternative? - What non-monetary things (reliability, references, flexibility) can I use to strengthen my position? - What should I avoid saying?
