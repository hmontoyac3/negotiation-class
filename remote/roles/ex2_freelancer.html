Exercise 2 · The Project · Your Role

THE
DESIGNER

You're a freelance UX/UI designer.
A health startup wants to hire you.
⏺ Before you start negotiating: hit RECORD on your video call. You'll need the recording for your submission.
Your Situation
  • You're a freelance UX/UI designer with 2 years of experience, building a niche in health-tech apps
  • A startup called Kura wants you to redesign their health app — a 8–10 week project
  • Your standard market rate for a project this size would be €8,000
  • Your BATNA: you have two other interested clients, but they're e-commerce — less interesting for your portfolio
  • You'd like to close this soon — you have a gap in your schedule next month
Your Numbers — Keep These Secret
€8,000 Your Anchor Standard market rate for this scope
€5,500 Your Minimum Below this, the e-commerce clients are better
What You Really Care About

More than anything, you want to add a health app to your portfolio. You're building a specialization in health-tech and a real client project — especially one with a clean brand like Kura — is worth a lot to your career. You'd estimate this portfolio value at €800–1,000. A deal at €5,800 with full portfolio rights beats €6,500 with no portfolio rights, in your calculation.

⚠ Do Not Reveal
  • That your minimum is €5,500
  • How much you want the portfolio rights — this is your hidden value, negotiate it strategically
  • That you have a schedule gap (suggests you're not in high demand right now)
Use AI to Prepare — 10 Minutes
Copy this prompt. Then ask a second question: "What might the startup care about or not care about beyond the fee?" — note if that changes your approach.
I'm a freelance UX/UI designer negotiating a fee for redesigning a health app startup's product. My standard rate for this scope is €8,000 but I'd accept as low as €5,500. I have other clients interested but prefer this one because it's in health-tech. The startup wants to negotiate the price down. Help me prepare: - What should my opening ask be and how do I justify it? - How do I hold my rate under pressure? - What do I do if they offer far below my minimum? - What should I avoid saying?
