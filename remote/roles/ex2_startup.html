THE
STARTUP

You need your app redesigned.
You're negotiating with a freelance designer.
⏺ Before you start negotiating: hit RECORD on your video call. You'll need the recording for your submission.
Your Situation
  • You're the founder of Kura, a health-tech startup. Your app needs a full UX/UI redesign — current design is losing users
  • Scope: complete redesign, 8–10 weeks of work, delivery of all Figma source files
  • You found a designer with a strong portfolio in health apps — your partner in this negotiation
  • Your BATNA: a cheaper junior designer at €4,500, but their health-app experience is limited and you're worried about quality
  • You need this done before your next funding round in 4 months
Your Budget — Keep This Secret
€7,000 Your Ceiling Approved budget — don't reveal this
€5,000 Your Target What you'd love to pay
Something You Haven't Thought About

You need the Figma source files and the finished screens. You have no opinion about portfolio rights — whether the designer can show this project publicly doesn't affect you at all. In fact, you'd welcome it: if they showcase Kura in their portfolio, it's free publicity for your brand.

⚠ Do Not Reveal
  • That your ceiling is €7,000 — they'll push right to it
  • That your junior alternative has limited health-app experience (it weakens your BATNA)
  • How urgent this is before the funding round
Use AI to Prepare — 10 Minutes
Copy this prompt. Then ask one more question after: "What might the designer care about beyond the fee?" — note if the answer changes your strategy.
I'm a startup founder hiring a freelance UX/UI designer to redesign my health app. My approved budget is €7,000 but I want to close as low as possible — ideally €5,000. I have a cheaper alternative but they're less experienced. The designer wants to negotiate. Help me prepare: - How do I keep the price down without losing the designer? - What arguments justify a lower fee? - How do I handle it if they anchor high (€8,000+)? - What should I avoid saying?
Your Submission (both partners submit together)
  • Recording of your negotiation call (unedited)
  • AI chat log — include the full conversation, especially if you asked "what might the other side care about beyond money?"
  • 3 lines together: What was the final deal (price + any other terms)? Did you discover a win-win? If yes, when?
