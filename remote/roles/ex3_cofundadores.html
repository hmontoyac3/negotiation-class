Exercise 3 · The Co-Founders · Group Briefing

THE
CO-FOUNDERS

Two friends are starting a company together. They need to agree on 4 things before they can begin. Your group's job is to analyze the situation and design the best possible agreement — for both of them.
35 min Total time for this exercise including video recording
The Two Founders
Founder A — Tech
  • Will build the entire product for the first 18 months
  • Has 12 months of savings — doesn't need a salary immediately
  • Worried about: the company pivoting away from the product vision without consulting them
  • Worried about: being diluted if B brings in investors
  • Position: 50-50 equity, equal decision-making
  • Real interest: Recognition for technical work, protection if vision changes, no financial pressure for 12 months
Founder B — Business
  • Will handle sales, fundraising, and operations
  • Has 4 months of savings — needs some income by month 5 or 6
  • Worried about: A leaving early and taking all the technical knowledge with them
  • Worried about: being seen as "less important" because they don't write code
  • Position: 50-50 equity, wants a small salary from month 4
  • Real interest: Financial security in the short term, protection if A leaves, equal respect and decision-making power
The 4 Issues They Need to Agree On
Issue 1
Equity Split
How do they divide ownership? 50-50? 60-40? Some other split? What arguments justify each option?
Issue 2
Vesting Schedule
What happens if one founder leaves after 6 months? After 18 months? A vesting schedule (earning equity over time) protects both sides — but how should it be structured?
Issue 3
Decision-Making
Who has final say when they disagree? On what topics? How do they prevent deadlock when they vote 1-1?
Issue 4
Salary
Do they pay themselves from day 1? How much? When does it start? Who gets paid first if the company only has money for one salary?
Your Group's Task — 4 People, 35 Minutes
1

Assign roles (2 min)

Two of you represent Founder A's perspective. Two represent Founder B's. You're not negotiating against each other — you're analyzing both sides to find the best agreement.

2

Use AI to understand both sides (10 min)

Each pair uses the AI prompts below to understand their founder's real interests, BATNA, and priorities. Share your findings with the full group.

3

Design the optimal agreement together (15 min)

For each of the 4 issues: What do both sides actually need? Is there a solution that satisfies both? Don't just split the difference — find the solution that creates the most value for both founders.

4

Record your 3-minute video (8 min)

All 4 members on camera. Present your proposed agreement and explain why it works for both founders using the concepts from the module (BATNA, interests vs. positions, win-win). Everyone must speak.

AI Prompts — One Per Pair
The A-pair and B-pair each use their prompt, then share findings with the full group before designing the agreement.
For the Founder A pair
Two friends are starting a tech startup. Founder A is the technical co-founder who will build the product. They have 12 months of personal savings and no urgent need for salary. They're worried about the company pivoting without their input, and about being diluted by investors. They want 50-50 equity and equal decision-making. Analyze Founder A's situation: - What is their BATNA (what happens if no deal is reached)? - What are their real interests vs. their stated position? - On salary, vesting, and decision-making: what outcome would actually serve them best? - Where might they be willing to give ground?
For the Founder B pair
Two friends are starting a tech startup. Founder B handles business: sales, fundraising, operations. They have only 4 months of personal savings and need some income by month 5-6 or they'll be in financial trouble. They're worried about the technical founder leaving early and taking all knowledge with them. They want 50-50 equity but also need a small salary from month 4. Analyze Founder B's situation: - What is their BATNA (what happens if no deal is reached)? - What are their real interests vs. their stated position? - On salary, vesting, and decision-making: what outcome would actually serve them best? - Where might they be willing to give ground?
Submission — One per group of 4
  • Video (3 minutes, all 4 on camera, everyone speaks) — your proposed agreement + why it works for both founders
  • AI chat logs from both pairs (screenshot or export)
  • One paragraph: Which issue was hardest to solve, and why?
The video is assessed on reasoning, not production quality. Phone camera is fine. What matters: can you explain WHY your agreement is good for both sides?
