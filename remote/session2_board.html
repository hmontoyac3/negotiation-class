WHAT THEY ACTUALLY NEEDED
The Startup — Kura

Needed the Figma files and finished screens. Had a budget up to €7,000.

Had zero interest in portfolio rights — actually would benefit from the designer showcasing the work. Free publicity for their brand.

The Designer

Minimum was €5,500 but the standard rate was €8,000.

Most important thing: portfolio rights for this health-tech project. Worth €800–1,000 to them in career value. A deal at €5,800 with portfolio rights beats €6,500 without them.

The Win-Win That Was Available from Minute One

Portfolio rights cost the startup nothing — they benefit from them. For the designer, they're worth more than €500 in extra fee.

A deal at €5,800 + full portfolio rights is better for both parties than fighting to €6,500 with no rights. The value was there the whole time — the only barrier was that neither side asked: "What do you care about beyond the money?"

THE DEBRIEF
This debrief has an extra section: the AI meta-lesson. It's the most important thing they take from this session.
LEVEL 1 — What happened?
Who found the win-win? When did you realize it existed — what minute of the negotiation?
For those who didn't find it — at what point did you realize after the reveal?
What was the first question you asked your partner?
LEVEL 2 — Why?
Both cards said "negotiate the price." Why did you assume price was the only variable?
What's the difference between a position ("I want €8,000") and an interest ("I want portfolio rights in health-tech")? Use this case.
The pairs who found the win-win — what did they do in the first 5 minutes that was different?
THE AI META-LESSON — most important
Your card said to ask AI: "What might the other side care about beyond the fee?" — who actually asked that? What did AI say?
The default prompt ("how do I get the best price?") and the better prompt produce completely different answers. Why?
What does this tell you about how to use AI before any negotiation? What's the question you should always ask?
LEVEL 3 — So what?
Give me a real example from your life where two people fought over something — but their actual needs didn't conflict.
Before your next important negotiation, what three things will you ask AI? Write them down right now.
💡 Key line to deliver: "AI gives you what you ask for. If you ask 'how do I win?' — you get competitive advice. If you ask 'what might the other side actually need?' — you get integrative insight. The tool amplifies your frame. It doesn't give you a better one. That's on you."
