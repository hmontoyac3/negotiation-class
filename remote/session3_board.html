LIVE PRESENTATIONS
Ask 3–4 groups to present their agreement live (2 min each). The rest listen and can react. You don't need to hear from every group — the video submissions cover them all.

What to ask after each group

"Why did you choose this equity split?" · "What would happen to Founder B if A left after 6 months with your vesting?" · "Which issue was hardest to agree on, and what broke the deadlock?"

What good looks like

Groups that name both founders' interests (not just positions), that design solutions not just compromises, and that explain how AI helped them think rather than think for them.

What to challenge

Groups that do 50-50 on everything without explanation. Ask: "Is 50-50 actually the best answer here, or just the easiest one?" Good groups will have a real answer.

💡 The best debrief moment: ask which groups had AI say something genuinely useful that they wouldn't have thought of on their own vs. which groups used AI to confirm what they already thought. Contrast them.
THE SYNTHESIS
Three sessions. Three exercises. Three concepts that never go away. Name them — and connect each to what they actually did.
1

BATNA & Reservation Price

Session 1 — The Flat. Your power comes from knowing your walk-away. No BATNA = no floor. Every pressure tactic works on you when you don't know your alternative.

2

Positions vs. Interests

Session 2 — The Project. What people ask for and what they need are almost never the same. The win-win always lives in the interests. Positions divide. Interests connect.

3

Design, Don't Fight

Session 3 — The Co-Founders. The best negotiators don't claim value — they create it. Understanding both sides deeply enough to design a better agreement is the highest-order skill.

3 Questions — Before Every Negotiation
Question 1
"What do I actually need — and what happens if I don't get it?"
Your BATNA and reservation price. Know them before you open your mouth. Without them, any pressure tactic works on you.
Question 2
"What do they actually need — and why do they need it?"
The integrative unlock. Their position is what they say. Their interest is why. Win-win lives in interests. If you don't know their interest, you're negotiating blind.
Question 3
"What can I give them that costs me little — but is worth a lot to them?"
Value creation, not just value claiming. The designer's portfolio rights. The startup's flexibility on start date. Your cheap is their precious. Look for asymmetry.
✓ Use AI to answer all three questions before any important negotiation. Then put it away — and show up as a human.
